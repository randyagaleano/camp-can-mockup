Assembly Instructions

Remove the top.

Carefully remove the top of an empty Camp Can by cutting along the lid’s inner groove with a sturdy knife or blade. Use the groove as a guide, and be extremeful careful!

Cut into thirds using guide.

Cut along the dashed lines on the Camp Can label to divide the can into thirds. Do not remove the Camp Can label just yet. You’ll need to use the remaining markings.

Create burner holes.

The dashed lines along of the can lip mark the burner holes. Use your blade to carefully make an incision along each line. Wiggle the blade horizontally to widen holes.

Remove label from top of can.

After the stove’s burner holes are cut, remove and discard the top of the can’s label. Use your blade to score or cut the label for easy removal. We’ll come back this piece later!

Make two short incisions.

There are two short, vertical dashed lines indicated on the bottom of the middle section. Gently make short incisions to the end of each dashed line. These will be your fuel vents.

Cut section top to bottom.

Cut the middle section from top to bottom along the remaining vertical, dashed line. You may now remove the can label from this section of your Cam Can Stove!

Make your gas vents!

Fold the aluminum near the fuel vent incisions outward to finish the vents. You’ve now created the inner wall of your Camp Can Stove! This piece is finished!

Crimp the top section.

Use the back of your blade to gently crimp the lower portion of the can’s top. The goal is to reduce the top’s diameter to allow it to fit inside the bottom third of the can.

Place middle inside top.

Insert the can’s middle section into the top. Wedge it into the interior of the lid seam to firmly secure. There should now be a nice gap between the middle and top pieces.

Complete top assembly.

The middle section will now be your interior wall. Make sure it is firmly secured into the top lid and there are no holes or gaps between the lid and inner walls of the stove.

Place top assembly into bottom section.

Insert the top and middle assembly into the bottom third of the can. You may need to wiggle the top or adjust your crimps slightly.

Ensure sections are snug.

The top assembly should fit snuggly by the time the interior wall reaches the bottom. Make sure there are no gaps between the bottom and top, but if there are a few, that’s ok!

Add fuel.

With an alcohol-based fuel, fill the stove about 1/3rd full—just past the top of the interior wall’s fuel vents. A great fuel is Heet, which is available at most gas stations for around $2.

Carefully light stove.

Ensure the stove is on a sturdy, level surface. Gently lower a flame into your Camp Can. Expect an audible ignition as the fumes will most likely ignite the stove almost immediately.

Let stove heat up.

The stove will begin to burn with a flame coming out of the center. Be careful! The flame will barely be visible. It’s there, and it’s hot!

Burner holes should ignite.

After a couple minutes, the fuel should enter the interior wall’s fuel vents and ignite the exterior burners. It may help to gently swirl the fuel in the stove to facilitate this.

Rest on sturdy, level surface, carefully apply
flame, and enjoy about 20 minutes of burn time.
